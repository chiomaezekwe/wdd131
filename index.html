Imo, Nigeria

Nigeria, Africa's most populous country with over 200 million people, is noted for its numerous cultures, languages, and rich history. Despite challenges like political instability and economic inequality, it remains a major player in Africa, driven by oil and agriculture.

Imo, a state located in the South-East geopolitical zone of Nigeria. It is bordered to the north by Anambra State, to the west and south by Rivers State, and to the east by Abia State. The state is named after the Imo River, which flows along its eastern border. The capital of Imo State is Owerri, and its slogan is - Eastern Heartland.
